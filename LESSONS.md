## Customer

- [L1] 2026-06-12 — Demand pools that deplete (one-time recoveries, lookbacks, arbitrage windows) are not markets. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L2] 2026-06-12 — Vertical beats horizontal in applied AI; demand concentrates where errors carry legal, financial, or safety consequences. _(from: idea-registry)_

## Distribution

- [L3] 2026-06-12 — A market with a handful of concentrated buyers fails distribution for a solo founder. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L4] 2026-06-12 — Total platform dependency is disqualifying, and a huge TAM does not cure it; if the platform owns the interface or registry, it owns you. _(from: idea-registry)_

## Sales

- [L5] 2026-06-12 — Long sales cycles kill solo founders; govtech and enterprise sell slowly no matter how good the product. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L6] 2026-06-12 — Enterprise trust requirements (SOC 2, E&O, vendor security review) wall off low-capital solo founders before product quality ever matters. _(from: idea-registry)_

## Pricing

- [L7] 2026-06-12 — Contingency and recovery revenue create services gravity; the recurring-subscription conversion is a separate startup that must be proven separately. _(from: idea-registry)_

## Competition

- [L8] 2026-06-12 — A category with named unicorns is closed to a low-capital founder. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L9] 2026-06-12 — Never compete where the leader grows faster than you can hire. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L10] 2026-06-12 — "Fragmented incumbents" is not enough if the startup layer attacking them is already crowded. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L11] 2026-06-12 — Features of platforms are not companies. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L12] 2026-06-12 — Standards-governed layers reward consortium members, not startups. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L13] 2026-06-12 — Assume your insight already has a cap table; search for competitors before building, not after. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L14] 2026-06-12 — An early-contested space beats a crowded one but loses to an empty one. _(from: idea-registry)_

## Moat

- [L15] 2026-06-12 — Check whether the real moat in a category is capital before entering it undercapitalized. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L16] 2026-06-12 — Archives are the AI-era moat: continuously collected historical data cannot be backfilled regardless of model quality, so time compounds it. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L17] 2026-06-12 — Acquisition pain is defensibility: fragmented, obscure, adversarially hard-to-gather data can moat; data published to be found cannot. _(from: idea-registry)_

## Regulation

- [L18] 2026-06-12 — Person-level data sold for screening triggers consumer-reporting regulation (FCRA); property-level data for property decisions stays clean. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L19] 2026-06-12 — Markets that need a crisis or new regulation to exist cannot be timed. _(from: idea-registry)_

## Execution

- [L20] 2026-06-12 — Respect the founder constraint, not just the market; an idea too big for a solo MVP is dead regardless of TAM. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L21] 2026-06-12 — Optimize the actual bottleneck (trust, licenses, signatures), not the 95% of work-hours that are already easy. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L22] 2026-06-12 — Agent org charts don't fix businesses whose constraint is human trust. _(from: idea-registry)_

## Founder Psychology

- [L23] 2026-06-12 — Founder economics differ from investor economics: a capital-light cash-flowing business can be great to start and a bad venture bet. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L24] 2026-06-12 — Know which game you're playing: a wonderful small business fails a $10B filter, and that only matters if you're playing the venture game. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L25] 2026-06-12 — The factory compounds; individual startups are allowed to fail. _(from: idea-registry)_

## AI Risk

- [L26] 2026-06-12 — Build complements to model progress, not applications of it: build what AI makes scarce, never what it makes cheap. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L27] 2026-06-12 — Never build where model progress arms incumbents equally; every model release strengthens whoever owns the scarce assets. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L28] 2026-06-12 — Don't sell what your supplier must build to survive (safety, evals, memory: the labs' own roadmap). _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L29] 2026-06-12 — Never bet against the generator improving; detection arms races are structurally lost. _(from: idea-registry)_
- [L30] 2026-06-12 — Avoid adversarial markets where the counterparty has more data and also has AI. _(from: idea-registry)_
